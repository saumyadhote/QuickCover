# QuickCover ‚ÄĒ Financial Model & AI Variable Micro-Charge Research

## Context

QuickCover is an income protection product for Blinkit delivery workers. The product is
**free to the driver** ‚ÄĒ protection is funded entirely by a micro-charge added to the
consumer's order at checkout. The consumer pays a tiny, transparent surcharge per order
(e.g. ‚āĻ2‚Äď5) which is pooled to cover driver income disruptions. This document grounds the
model in real earnings data, disruption frequency, and consumer-side viable premium economics.

## 4. The QuickCover Financial Model

### Core Revenue Mechanism ‚ÄĒ Consumer-Funded Micro-Charge

> The driver pays nothing. Every consumer who places a Blinkit order contributes a small
> surcharge that is pooled into a protection fund. When a verified disruption occurs,
> affected drivers are automatically compensated from the pool.

This mirrors models like:
- **Swiggy's "Rain Fee"** ‚ÄĒ consumers already accept small surcharges during bad weather
- **Carbon offset add-ons** ‚ÄĒ opt-in/opt-out framing at checkout
- **Delivery tip pooling** ‚ÄĒ consumers comfortable directing small amounts to drivers

### The Consumer Micro-Charge

| Order Value | QuickCover Surcharge | As % of Order |
|---|---|---|
| ‚āĻ100‚Äď‚āĻ300 (small basket) | ‚āĻ2 | 0.7‚Äď2% |
| ‚āĻ300‚Äď‚āĻ700 (typical basket) | ‚āĻ3 | 0.4‚Äď1% |
| ‚āĻ700‚Äď‚āĻ1,500 (large basket) | ‚āĻ5 | 0.3‚Äď0.7% |

**Average charge per order: ‚āĻ3** ‚ÄĒ less than the cost of a single Mentos.

Blinkit avg order value: ~‚āĻ500‚Äď‚āĻ600. At ‚āĻ3/order this is **0.5% of order value** ‚ÄĒ
well within the range consumers accept for delivery fees and surcharges.

### AI Variable Charge Factors

The AI engine adjusts the per-order surcharge in real time based on:

1. **Weather risk score** ‚ÄĒ IMD rainfall forecast for the delivery zone
2. **Zone disruption risk** ‚ÄĒ historical disruption frequency for that pin code
3. **Active driver shortage** ‚ÄĒ fewer available drivers = higher protection need
4. **Time-of-day factor** ‚ÄĒ night deliveries carry higher risk
5. **Platform demand surge** ‚ÄĒ high demand during disruption = more workers exposed
6. **Cumulative pool balance** ‚ÄĒ if pool is well-funded, surcharge drops; if thin, it rises

Formula concept:
```
order_surcharge = base_rate √ó weather_multiplier √ó zone_multiplier √ó pool_balance_factor
```

Example:
- Clear weather, healthy pool: ‚āĻ2.00 (minimum)
- Heavy rain warning active, pool running low: ‚āĻ5.00 (maximum)
- Consumer always sees it clearly at checkout ‚ÄĒ full transparency

### Payout Triggers (Parametric ‚ÄĒ Automatic, No Driver Claim Needed)

| Trigger | Condition | Payout to Driver |
|---|---|---|
| Heavy rain | IMD: >15mm/hr in delivery zone | ‚āĻ300‚Äď500 per disrupted shift |
| Extreme heat | Temp >43¬įC for 2+ hrs during shift | ‚āĻ250‚Äď400 per shift |

| Trip accident | Self-reported + location anomaly | Up to ‚āĻ2,000 (capped) |

Payouts credited automatically to the driver's UPI / platform wallet.
**Driver never files a claim. Driver never pays a premium.**

### Weekly Coverage Cap Per Driver

## 6. Why Consumer-Funded is the Right Model

### vs. Driver-Paid Premium

| | Driver Pays | Consumer Funds |
|---|---|---|
| Driver adoption | Requires convincing drivers to spend | Instant ‚ÄĒ zero cost to driver |
| Adverse selection | Only worried drivers sign up | Universal coverage for all active drivers |
| Income sensitivity | Driver may drop coverage on bad month | Unaffected ‚ÄĒ consumer pays regardless |
| Moral framing | Insurance product | Social impact surcharge ("protect your rider") |
| Scalability | Capped by driver willingness to pay | Scales with order volume |

### Consumer Psychology

- **‚āĻ3 is invisible** at the point of a ‚āĻ500+ grocery order
- Framing matters: "‚āĻ3 protects your delivery partner during today's storm" converts better
  than a generic fee
- Opt-out (default on) vs opt-in: opt-out dramatically increases participation
- Precedent: consumers already accept rain fees, surge pricing, packaging charges

### vs. Platform-Provided Insurance (Blinkit's own)

- QuickCover is **portable** ‚ÄĒ works across Blinkit, Zepto, Swiggy (multi-platform roadmap)
- Platform insurance doesn't cover weather/outage income loss ‚ÄĒ QuickCover does
- Platform insurance has documented claim denial issues ‚ÄĒ parametric = automatic, no denial
- QuickCover creates a **brand differentiator** for Blinkit ("we care about our partners")

## 7. Regulatory Path

- Falls under IRDAI (Insurance Products) Regulations 2024 ‚ÄĒ "innovative insurance products"
- Micro-insurance exemption for products <‚āĻ50,000 annual premium per beneficiary
- QuickCover operates as a **distribution / technology layer** on top of a licensed insurer
  (no own IRDAI license required initially ‚ÄĒ partner with a licensed insurer who underwrites)
- The consumer surcharge is structured as a **protection fund contribution**, not an insurance
  premium paid by the consumer (consumer is donor, driver is beneficiary)
- Government's Central Social Security Fund for gig workers creates strong policy tailwind
